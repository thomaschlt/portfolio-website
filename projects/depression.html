Mental Health study



Languages :

R

duration :

February 2021 - March 2021

links :

Ressources

This study aims to investigate the impact of the Internet on mental illness. The project uses R for data analysis, and employs both descriptive and inferential statistical methods as well as a technique called principal component analysis (PCA). Data is collected through a survey using the CES-D (Center for Epidemiologic Studies-Depression) scale, which measures the level of depression symptoms in individuals. The study aims to explore the relationship between Internet usage and depression symptoms, and to identify potential risk factors associated with the development of depression among internet users. The results of this study could provide valuable insights into the potential negative effects of excessive Internet use and inform the development of interventions to support individuals at risk.
